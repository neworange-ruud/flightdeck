# Pair Your Phone

Pairing establishes the end-to-end-encrypted link between FlightDeck Remote and
your Mac. You only do it once per device; the pairing persists across app
launches until you unpair.

![The pairing screen: 'Pair with your Mac', a 4-digit code entry, and 'Scan QR instead'.](/screenshots/mobile/01-pairing.png)

*The pairing screen — scan the QR shown on your Mac, or type the 4-digit code.*

## On your Mac

1. Make sure FlightDeck Remote is enabled on the desktop. It's **off by
   default** — set `enabled = true` under `[remote]` in your config, or just run
   the next step, which turns it on.
2. Open the command palette (`Ctrl-g`) and run **Pair Phone**. FlightDeck shows
   a pairing overlay with a **QR code**, a **4-digit code**, and a live expiry
   countdown.

## On your phone

Open FlightDeck Remote. While unpaired, it opens on the **Pair with your Mac**
screen. Choose either path:

- **Scan QR** — tap **Scan QR instead** and point the camera at the QR code on
  your Mac.
- **Enter the code** — type the 4-digit code into the four boxes and tap
  **Pair**.

When pairing succeeds, the desktop overlay shows **Paired ✓** and the phone
moves straight to the [Projects](/remote/monitoring) screen.

<Callout type="success">
  Everything after pairing is end-to-end encrypted. The footer on the pairing
  screen says it plainly: **"End-to-end encrypted · unlocked by Face ID."**
</Callout>

## Why pairing matters

Pairing is the real trust decision: once paired, your phone can send commands
that run on your Mac. The QR/code carries the secret that bootstraps the
encrypted channel, and your device registers an identity keypair whose private
half stays in the iOS Keychain / Secure Enclave. The relay authenticates devices
by key and routes only by pairing ID — it can never read your traffic.

<Callout type="note">
  If the camera isn't available (for example in the Simulator) or permission is
  denied, the app shows **"Camera unavailable — enter the code instead"** with a
  button to switch to code entry.
</Callout>

## Bad codes and errors

Entering the wrong code shows an inline error and a brief shake, and keeps you on
the pairing screen so you can try again. The **Pair** button stays disabled until
you've entered all four digits.

## Unpairing

To disconnect a phone, open **Settings** in the app and tap **Unpair this
device** (see [Settings & Security](/remote/settings)). On the desktop, run
**Unpair Phone** from the command palette. After unpairing you'll need to pair
again to reconnect.
