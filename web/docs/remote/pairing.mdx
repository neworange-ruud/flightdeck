# Pair Your Phone

Pairing establishes the end-to-end-encrypted link between FlightDeck Remote and
your Mac. You do it once per Mac; the pairing persists across app launches until
you unpair. One phone can be paired with **up to four Macs** at once — see
[Pairing more than one Mac](#pairing-more-than-one-mac).

![The pairing screen: 'Pair with your Mac', a 4-digit code entry, and 'Scan QR instead'.](/screenshots/mobile/01-pairing.png)

*The pairing screen — scan the QR shown on your Mac, or type the 4-digit code.*

## On your Mac

1. Make sure FlightDeck Remote is enabled on the desktop. It's **off by
   default** — set `enabled = true` under `[remote]` in your config, or just run
   the next step, which turns it on.
2. Open the command palette (`Ctrl-g`) and run **Pair Phone**. FlightDeck shows
   a pairing overlay with a **QR code**, a **4-digit code**, and a live expiry
   countdown.

## On your phone

Open FlightDeck Remote. While unpaired, it opens on the **Pair with your Mac**
screen. Choose either path:

- **Scan QR** — tap **Scan QR instead** and point the camera at the QR code on
  your Mac.
- **Enter the code** — type the 4-digit code into the four boxes and tap
  **Pair**.

When pairing succeeds, the desktop overlay shows **Paired ✓** and the phone
moves straight to the [Feed](/remote/feed).

<Callout type="success">
  Everything after pairing is end-to-end encrypted. The footer on the pairing
  screen says it plainly: **"End-to-end encrypted · unlocked by Face ID."**
</Callout>

## Why pairing matters

Pairing is the real trust decision: once paired, your phone can send commands
that run on your Mac. The QR/code carries the secret that bootstraps the
encrypted channel, and your device registers an identity keypair whose private
half stays in the iOS Keychain / Secure Enclave. The relay authenticates devices
by key and routes only by pairing ID — it can never read your traffic.

<Callout type="note">
  If the camera isn't available (for example in the Simulator) or permission is
  denied, the app shows **"Camera unavailable — enter the code instead"** with a
  button to switch to code entry.
</Callout>

## Bad codes and errors

Entering the wrong code shows an inline error and a brief shake, and keeps you on
the pairing screen so you can try again. The **Pair** button stays disabled until
you've entered all four digits.

## Pairing more than one Mac

One phone can drive several Macs — a laptop and a desktop, home and work — from a
single app. Once you're paired with your first Mac, add another from either:

- the **`+`** in the [Feed](/remote/feed) header, or
- **Add machine** in **Settings → Machines**.

Both open the same pair-a-Mac flow (QR or 4-digit code) as a sheet; on the
desktop you're adding, run **Pair Phone** exactly as before. The new Mac appears
in the Feed the moment it connects, and its projects interleave with the rest.

<Callout type="info">
  A phone can be paired with **up to four Macs**. At the limit the pairing
  controls are disabled and the app explains why: **"You've reached the limit of
  4 paired Macs. Unpair one before adding another."** The **Add machine** row in
  Settings shows the live count (e.g. *3/4*).
</Callout>

All your Macs share the phone's single device identity — adding a Mac never
generates new keys, and unpairing one leaves the others' keys untouched.

## Naming your Macs

Each Mac reports its own name (its computer name), shown on the machine chip in
the Feed and in **Settings → Machines**. To override it — handy when two Macs
have similar names — tap the machine's row in **Settings → Machines** and use
**Rename machine**. Your override always wins; **Reset to Mac's name** clears it
and falls back to what the desktop reports. Renaming on the desktop updates the
reported name automatically.

## Unpairing

You can unpair **one** Mac or **all** of them:

- **One Mac** — open **Settings → Machines** and tap the red minus (–) on its
  row, then confirm. This removes it from the phone and revokes it on your Mac;
  your other Macs stay paired.
- **This device entirely** — **Unpair this device** in **Settings → Security**
  drops every pairing and returns you to the pairing screen.

On the desktop side, run **Unpair Phone** from the command palette. After
unpairing a Mac you'll need to pair it again to reconnect. See
[Settings & Security](/remote/settings) for the full Settings layout.
