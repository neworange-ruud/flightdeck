# Activity & Notifications

FlightDeck Remote keeps you in the loop two ways: a persistent **Activity feed**
inside the app, and **push notifications** that reach you even when the app is
closed.

## The Activity feed

The **Activity** tab is a newest-first feed of status events from your paired
Mac: an agent **needs input** (orange), **finished** (green), or hit an **error**
(red).

![The Activity feed: needs-input, finished, and error event cells.](/screenshots/mobile/10-activity.png)

*Every needs-input, finished, and error event, newest first — each deep-links to its agent.*

Each cell shows the event, a short message, and the project it belongs to. Tap a
cell to jump straight to that agent's [chat](/remote/chat). (Tapping an event for
a session that no longer exists tells you so and stays put.)

The Activity tab carries an **unread dot** when there are new events; it clears
as soon as you open the tab.

## Push notifications

The same events arrive as push notifications when you're away from the app,
delivered through Apple Push Notification service via the relay.

- **Needs input** — a time-sensitive, distinctly urgent alert,
- **Finished** — e.g. *"18 files changed · ready to push"* with a standard chime,
- **Error** — a chime.

Tapping any notification deep-links straight to the agent. Your **home-screen
badge** shows the number of agents currently waiting for input.

<Callout type="note">
  Notification permission is requested only **after pairing** — pairing is the
  trust step. The relay's push is zero-knowledge: it carries only routing
  metadata, never your content.
</Callout>

## Choosing what notifies you

In [Settings](/remote/settings) you control notifications with three independent
toggles — **Agent needs input**, **Agent finished**, and **Completion chime**
(all on by default; the chime is independent, so you can be notified silently).

You can also **mute individual projects**: a *"Mute by project"* card lists your
projects, each with its own toggle, so a noisy project can be silenced without
affecting the rest.

## Staying honest offline

When your phone can't reach the relay, the app shows your **last-known state**,
clearly marked stale with a *"Showing last-known state — offline"* banner, and
disables actions until the link returns. See [Settings & Security](/remote/settings)
for more on the offline cache and connection status.
