# Settings, Security & Offline

The **Settings** tab is where you check the connection, lock the app, tune
notifications, and unpair. This page also covers the security model and how the
app behaves offline.

![The Settings screen: connection card, security (Face ID, unpair), and notification toggles.](/screenshots/mobile/11-settings.png)

*Settings — connection status, security, and notification controls in one place.*

## Connection

The **Connection** card names your paired Mac and shows the live link status with
honest latency — *"Connected · 8ms · low latency"*, *"Connecting…"*, or
*"Offline."* It also shows when you paired.

When the link drops, a **Reconnecting** banner appears at the top of the app with
*"Commands are paused until the link is back. Nothing is sent blind."* After a
sustained outage it adds *"Still trying — is FlightDeck running on your Mac?"*

## App lock (Face ID)

Turn on **Require Face ID to open** in the Security section to gate the whole app
behind biometrics. When enabled, a lock screen covers everything on launch and
after the app has been backgrounded.

![The app lock screen requiring Face ID before revealing the app.](/screenshots/mobile/12-app-lock.png)

*Optional Face ID lock — an extra gate on top of pairing.*

<Callout type="note">
  App lock uses device authentication with a **passcode fallback**, and it
  **fails open** if your device has no passcode set at all — so it can never
  brick you out of the app. It's independent of pairing.
</Callout>

## Notifications

The Security card is followed by the notification controls covered in
[Activity & Notifications](/remote/activity): the three independent toggles
(**Agent needs input**, **Agent finished**, **Completion chime**) and the
per-project **Mute by project** list.

## Unpair

**Unpair this device** (in red) disconnects the phone from your Mac after a
confirmation. You'll return to the [pairing screen](/remote/pairing) and need to
pair again to reconnect.

## The security model

- Your device holds an **identity keypair** whose private key lives in the iOS
  Keychain / Secure Enclave and never leaves the phone.
- All traffic between phone and Mac is **end-to-end encrypted**; the hosted relay
  routes opaque ciphertext by pairing ID and cannot read it.
- **Pairing** is the moment of trust — after it, your phone can send commands
  that run on your Mac, so only pair with a code/QR you can see on your own
  machine.

## Offline behavior

When disconnected, the app shows your **cached last-known snapshot and
transcripts, read-only**, behind a muted *"Showing last-known state — offline"*
banner (distinct from the louder Reconnecting banner). No commands are sent while
offline — reads stay available, but every action waits for the link to return.
