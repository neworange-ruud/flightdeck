# Settings, Security & Offline

The **Settings** tab is where you check the connection, manage your paired Macs,
lock the app, tune notifications, and unpair. This page also covers the security
model and how the app behaves offline.

![The Settings screen: connection card, machines list with rename/mute/unpair, security, and notification toggles.](/screenshots/mobile/11-settings.png)

*Settings — connection, your machines, security, and notification controls in one place.*

## Connection

The **Connection** card names the Mac you're focused on and shows the live link
status with honest latency — *"Connected · 8ms · low latency"*, *"Connecting…"*,
or *"Offline."* It also shows when you paired.

## Machines

The **Machines** card lists every Mac you're paired with — one row each, with an
online/offline dot and the machine's display name. Each row lets you:

- **Rename it** — tap the row to open **Rename machine** and set a name that
  overrides what the desktop reports (**Reset to Mac's name** clears the
  override). See [Naming your Macs](/remote/pairing#naming-your-macs).
- **Mute its notifications** — the bell toggle silences push from just that Mac,
  without touching the others.
- **Unpair it** — the red minus (–) removes that one Mac after a confirmation and
  revokes it on the desktop; your other Macs stay paired.

Below the list, **Add machine** [pairs another Mac](/remote/pairing#pairing-more-than-one-mac)
and shows the live count against the limit (e.g. *3/4*). At four it's disabled.

When the link drops, a **Reconnecting** banner appears at the top of the app with
*"Commands are paused until the link is back. Nothing is sent blind."* After a
sustained outage it adds *"Still trying — is FlightDeck running on your Mac?"*

## App lock (Face ID)

Turn on **Require Face ID to open** in the Security section to gate the whole app
behind biometrics. When enabled, a lock screen covers everything on launch and
after the app has been backgrounded.

![The app lock screen requiring Face ID before revealing the app.](/screenshots/mobile/12-app-lock.png)

*Optional Face ID lock — an extra gate on top of pairing.*

<Callout type="note">
  App lock uses device authentication with a **passcode fallback**, and it
  **fails open** if your device has no passcode set at all — so it can never
  brick you out of the app. It's independent of pairing.
</Callout>

## Notifications

The Security card is followed by the notification controls covered in
[The Feed & Notifications](/remote/feed): the three independent toggles
(**Agent needs input**, **Agent finished**, **Completion chime**) and the
per-project **Mute by project** list. To silence a whole Mac, use the bell toggle
on its row in the [Machines](#machines) card above.

## Unpair

**Unpair this device** (in red) disconnects the phone from **every** Mac after a
confirmation and returns you to the [pairing screen](/remote/pairing). To drop
just one Mac and keep the rest, use the red minus (–) on its row in the
[Machines](#machines) card instead.

## The security model

- Your device holds an **identity keypair** whose private key lives in the iOS
  Keychain / Secure Enclave and never leaves the phone.
- All traffic between phone and Mac is **end-to-end encrypted**; the hosted relay
  routes opaque ciphertext by pairing ID and cannot read it. Each paired Mac is a
  separate encrypted channel, but they all share the phone's one device identity
  — unpairing one Mac never disturbs the others' keys.
- **Pairing** is the moment of trust — after it, your phone can send commands
  that run on your Mac, so only pair with a code/QR you can see on your own
  machine.

## Offline behavior

When disconnected, the app shows your **cached last-known snapshot and
transcripts, read-only**, behind a muted *"Showing last-known state — offline"*
banner (distinct from the louder Reconnecting banner). No commands are sent while
offline — reads stay available, but every action waits for the link to return.
