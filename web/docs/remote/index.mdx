# FlightDeck Remote

**FlightDeck Remote** is the companion iOS app for FlightDeck. Pair it to your
Mac — or to **several Macs at once** — and you can watch every agent, answer
their questions, run a shell, and drive guarded Git actions — from your phone,
wherever you are.

![The FlightDeck Remote projects screen and an agent conversation on iPhone.](/screenshots/mobile/02-projects-list.png) ![An agent conversation with a live permission prompt.](/screenshots/mobile/07-agent-chat.png)

*FlightDeck Remote — your agent fleet in your pocket.*

<Callout type="info">
  FlightDeck Remote is **iPhone-only** and requires **iOS 18 or later**. It has
  a dark, mission-control look throughout.
</Callout>

## What you can do from your phone

- **Monitor** — see every project and agent across all your paired Macs in one
  [Feed](/remote/feed), with their status and Git state at a glance; get
  [push notifications](/remote/feed) when an agent finishes or needs you.
- **Chat** — read a cleaned-up transcript, reply, and approve or deny permission
  prompts inline — including hands-free in [Focus mode](/remote/chat).
- **Control** — [start new agents](/remote/control), restart them, set a manual
  status, and run guarded Git actions (pull base, merge back, abandon).
- **Shell** — open a [live terminal](/remote/shell) in any worktree with an
  on-screen key bar.

## How it connects

Your phone never talks to your Mac directly. Both connect to a **hosted relay**,
and everything between them is **end-to-end encrypted**: the relay only routes
opaque ciphertext by pairing ID and never holds the keys to read it. When you
pair several Macs, each is its own encrypted channel — but they all share the
one device identity on your phone, so adding or removing a Mac never touches the
others.

<Callout type="success">
  Pairing is the trust step — it establishes the encrypted channel between your
  phone and your Mac. Your device's private key lives in the iOS Keychain /
  Secure Enclave and never leaves the phone.
</Callout>

## Availability

<Callout type="warning">
  **The default relay is not open to the public.** FlightDeck ships configured to
  use a hosted relay (`relay.flightdeckai.app`), but that relay is currently
  **restricted** — it does not accept connections from the general public, so
  enabling Remote against the default URL will not connect. You are free to
  **host your own relay** and point FlightDeck at it (set `relay_url` under
  `[remote]` in your config, or edit it in the configuration manager), but
  **self-hosting is not supported by the author in any way**.
</Callout>

To use it you'll:

1. **Enable Remote on the desktop** — it's off by default. Set `enabled = true`
   under `[remote]` in your FlightDeck config, or run *Pair Phone* from the
   command palette. Remember the default relay is [not publicly
   accessible](#availability) — set `relay_url` to your own relay first.
2. **[Pair your phone](/remote/pairing)** — scan a QR code or type a 4-digit
   code. Repeat on each Mac you want to drive (up to four).

## What's not on the phone (v1)

Pushing branches and opening PRs stay on the desktop / your agent (the app holds
no GitHub token). Also out of scope for v1: several side-by-side shells, and
full-screen terminal UIs (vim, top). Agents only run while FlightDeck is running
on your Mac.
